## LinkedIn

A few months ago, I realized I kept losing things.

I'd read something genuinely brilliant about AI — a thread, a half-finished post, a message buried in some Discord — and a week later I could never find it again.

The best thinking about the most important technology of our lifetime is scattered across five platforms, disappearing as fast as it shows up.

That bothered me more than it probably should have.

So I built **Openpaper** — a real home for AI writing.

Not another generic blog. A place that actually understands what people building with AI are trying to say.

Here, an article can carry:
→ a prompt you copy in one tap
→ a model's output, with the version and date it came from
→ an @mention of a model that links to its own living page
→ a "freshness" stamp — because in this field, something from six months ago can already be history

Every piece is an article, a tutorial, or a benchmark — because those are the things people actually write.

And it's not built for spectators. It's for the people in the work: the prompt engineer who cracked something at 2am, the founder who shipped what was impossible last year, the researcher who ran the benchmark everyone's arguing about.

It's early. A little quiet. And honestly, that's fine — every real thing starts with one person publishing one honest piece.

The AI era is being written right now, by us, in real time. I just wanted it to live somewhere it won't disappear.

If you're building something, come write it down. I'll be reading. 👇

🔗 https://openpaper-five.vercel.app
