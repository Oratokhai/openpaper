## Status: 🔵 Tier 2 — Build in Beta

## What It Is

A pre-built, curated tag/category system that maps the actual AI landscape. Writers don't invent tags from scratch — they tag into a shared, searchable system that the whole Openpaper community uses.

---

## Why It Matters

Generic platforms have generic tags — "technology", "programming", "AI" — too broad to be useful for discovery. The AI space has rich, specific vocabulary. A taxonomy built by people who understand AI gives readers meaningful discovery and gives writers a shared language.

---

## Proposed Top-Level Categories

| Category | Covers |
|---|---|
| Models & Research | Model releases, paper analysis, benchmarks, evals |
| Prompt Engineering | Prompting techniques, system prompts, prompt patterns |
| RAG & Memory | Retrieval-augmented generation, vector DBs, context management |
| Agents & Automation | AI agents, tool use, multi-agent systems, workflows |
| Fine-tuning & Training | LoRA, RLHF, dataset curation, training infrastructure |
| LLM Apps & Products | Building with AI APIs, product reviews, app architecture |
| Infrastructure | GPU, inference, deployment, serving, latency |
| Multimodal | Vision, audio, video, image generation |
| AI Ethics & Policy | Safety, alignment, regulation, bias, responsible AI |
| Industry & Business | AI companies, funding, strategy, market analysis |
| Tools & Platforms | Reviews and comparisons of AI tools |
| Tutorials & Guides | Step-by-step, how-to, beginner to advanced |

---

## Rules

- Writer picks from the taxonomy — they don't type free-form tags (at MVP)
- Multiple categories allowed per article
- Readers can follow any category — articles tagged with it appear in their feed
- Categories are maintained by Openpaper — not community-editable
