# Why I built Openpaper

*Published 2026-06-02 by @oratokhai · /oratokhai/why-i-built-openpaper · the first real editorial post on the platform.*

**Subtitle:** The AI era is being written right now. I wanted it to live somewhere it won't disappear.

---

A few months ago I kept losing things. I'd read something genuinely good about AI, a thread, a half-written post, a message buried in some Discord, and a week later I could never find it again. The best thinking about the most important technology of our lifetime was scattered across five platforms and vanishing as fast as it showed up.

That bothered me more than it probably should have.

We are living through the AI era as it happens. People are working out how to build with these models right now, tonight, in their editors and terminals. They are finding things nobody has written down yet. And most of it just disappears. A tweet scrolls away. A long post gets a single read. A Discord message sinks to the bottom. The signal is everywhere and it lives nowhere.

So I built Openpaper. The idea is almost embarrassingly simple. Give AI writing a real home. Not another generic blog, but a place that actually understands what people building with AI are trying to say.

That is why an article here can carry a prompt you can copy in one tap, a model output you can expand or collapse, a mention of a model that links straight to its own living page. It is why every piece is an article, a tutorial, or a benchmark, because those are the things people actually write. It is why there is a freshness stamp on a post, because in this field something from six months ago can already be history, and you deserve to know that before you trust it.

I did not want to build a place for spectators. I wanted a place for the people in the work. The prompt engineer who cracked something at 2am. The founder who shipped a thing that would have been impossible last year. The researcher who ran the benchmark everyone is arguing about. If that is you, this was built for you.

Openpaper is small right now. It is early, and a little quiet, and that is honestly fine. Every real thing starts the same way, with one person publishing one honest piece, and then someone else reading it and quietly thinking, I could write mine too.

That is the whole bet. The AI era is being written right now, by us, in real time. I just wanted it to live somewhere it would not disappear.

So if you are building something, write it down here. I will be reading.
